## What this is

Witness Engine is not a chatbot demo and not a case file.

It is an exhibit about a new form of collaboration: a human who lived through a record works with a language model to aggregate, compress, test, and clarify that record without surrendering authorship of the experience.

The human brings continuity, memory, vulnerability, lived risk, and moral authority.

The model brings aggregation, compression, contradiction-mapping, language discipline, and the ability to hold many documents beside each other without fatigue.

Neither replaces the other.

The model is not the witness. The human is not reduced to data. The collaboration builds an engine for witness.

## The central idea

A record can be accurate sentence by sentence and still mislead by structure.

It can record pain without letting pain govern.

It can record uncertainty while acting with certainty.

It can record a person and still make that person fight to become audible.

## The method

Witness Engine asks four questions:

1. What was the first signal?
2. What became the organising frame?
3. Which recorded facts were subordinated?
4. Which reading of the documents matches the lived continuity?

## Competition framing

This project is built for a human/LLM mutual-understanding competition because it demonstrates a collaboration neither party could complete alone.

The human supplies the reality that cannot be simulated.

The model supplies a disciplined instrument for making that reality harder to misread.

The result is not artificial empathy.

The result is public legibility.

## Editorial position

This project is reflective and documentary. It is not medical advice, legal advice, or a final adjudication. It demonstrates how a human and a language model can make a complex record more legible without confusing legibility with lived knowledge.
