Closing essay

I do not know what it was like

The ethical boundary of the collaboration: a model can help make the record legible. It cannot know the life lived inside the record.

Opening limit

Legibility is not rescue.

There is a professional danger in speaking too fluently about another person’s suffering. Fluency can imitate understanding. It can arrange pain into sequence, translate injury into chronology, and make the whole thing sound manageable because it has finally been made legible.

But a coherent account is not the same thing as knowledge. The record can know dates, diagnoses, referrals, medication changes, omissions, correspondence, silence, escalation, and closure. It can know all of that and still not know what it was like to live there.

The sentence

There is a particular violence in being partially believed.

Total erasure is blunt. Partial preservation is more difficult. It gives the appearance of being heard while withholding the consequence of hearing.

Human scale

The record preserves the event, not the attrition.

It can say pain was prolonged, uncontrolled, debilitating, functionally limiting. It can say quality of life was affected. It can say psychological deterioration followed. But it cannot reproduce the lived arithmetic of a life reduced to essential movement.

It cannot measure the private humiliations of needing to plan around one's own body. It cannot capture the resentment of discovering that the body had become both prison and evidence.

The machine can say that. It can analyse it. It can even make it sound clean. But it does not know what it was like to live inside it.
